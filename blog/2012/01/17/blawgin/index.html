Introducing Roam Mobility'

Jan 17th, 2012

Finally, a great way to Roam.

This really is the best idea I’ve heard in a long time.

Roam Mobility is an operator that provides Canadians with a pay-as-you-go US phone number. They are really reasonably priced and you only need to use the service once a year to maintain your US number.

I was travelling in the US recently and Having this service would have saved me about $50 – that is just one trip. If you are a frequent traveller you should look into both their data and voice services.

I’ve read some comments in forums where people are questioning the value provided by the service and instead suggest that you’re better off dealing directly with US Carriers. I have used pay-as-you-go services from AT&T and T-Mobile – I think Roam is providing a much simpler and more cost effective way in the long run. On my last trip I even tried to get Verizon to Activate a “Verizon Jetpack” for me – they couldn’t do it because their 4G network is only available on contract.

Just a word to the wise, their partner is T-Mobile – this means you need a phone with AWS to get 3G/3.5G/3.667G/3.9999G/4.01G/etc. Your iPhone will roam on their Edge network but it will be slow. You should pick up a Nexus S with AWS (they go for about $350 these days but DO NOT have Rogers/Bell/Telus 3G) or a Galaxy Nexus (double the price at around $700 but works with everyone’s 3G) if you are looking for a phone to use.
